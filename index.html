About Myself

I am a computer engineering student at the University of Ottawa, who is new to user interface design.
I grew up in Kingston, ON, going to KCVI for high school. I then moved to Banff for a winter to live my dream of snowboarding everyday before starting my University journey at Dalhousie University. After two years in engineering at Dalhousie, I decided it was not for me and transferred to the University of Ottawa for computer engineering. I have spent the last two years in Ottawa studying and learning many topics related to computers and technology.
Since I am new to UI/UX design, I started this project by researching and watching crash courses on YouTube about HTMl, CSS and Bootstrap 5. Once I gained some knowledge and understanding of the basics, I started researching and looking about other UI/UX designers portfolios to gain some insipration for my first draft of my portfolio website.

Websites I took inspiration from:

Website 1
Website 2
Website 3
These portfolio websites inspired the themes, layout and overall look and feel of my portfolio website. More specifically, these three websites all have a minimalistic and clean design with easy navigation that encourages the user to scroll down through the website to discover the page. I also like how all three websites incorporate a navigation bar at the top of the screen. Right now I only have a basic navigation bar, but I plan on adding more to it, such as a possible drop down menu.
I then started drafting up my portfolio website in VS Code and continued to revise and improve it until I was satisfied with the final product. This took multiple revisions and a couple restarts at first, as the initial UI design was not what I had visioned. I am happy with the design I came up with, but I know that every website will continue to need adjustments in the future, so I am always open to feedback and I hope you enjoy my portfolio website!
